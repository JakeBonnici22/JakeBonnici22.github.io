The idea

Betting markets are sharp — but not perfect.

Bookmakers price events with an embedded margin (the vig), so the implied probabilities you read off the odds always sum to more than 100%. Strip the vig out and you get the market's honest estimate of who wins. The whole system is built around one question: can a calibrated model find matches where its probability is meaningfully higher than the market's? When it can, that gap is a positive-expected-value edge — and the system bets it, carefully.

How the model works

Good features, honest probabilities, no leakage.

Feature engineering

  • Elo ratings — dynamic per-player/team strength, updated after every match.
  • Surface-specific Elo (tennis) — separate Hard / Clay / Grass ratings.
  • Rolling form — win rate, sets/goals, and goal difference over recent matches.
  • Head-to-head — historical matchup win rates.
  • Market signals — vig-removed implied probabilities from sharp bookmakers.

The model

  • XGBoost with early stopping and regularisation.
  • Isotonic calibration so a predicted 60% really means 60% — essential when you bet on the probability itself.
  • Time-series split — always trained on the past, tested on the future. No look-ahead leakage.
  • Soccer is 3-class (home / draw / away); tennis is binary (player 1 wins).

Finding value & sizing risk

An edge is only worth betting if you stake it sensibly.

Value is the gap between the model's probability and the market's true (vig-removed) probability:

edge = model_probability − vig_removed_market_probability

Only matches with an edge of at least 4% become candidates. Each qualifying bet is then staked with fractional Kelly — the mathematically optimal growth rule, deliberately scaled down to tame variance:

f* = 0.25 × (b·p − q) / b cap = 3.0% of bankroll per bet floor = 0.5% of bankroll per bet

Quarter-Kelly with a hard 3% cap keeps drawdowns survivable: the system is built to compound slowly, not to swing for the fences on any single match.

Discipline & honesty

Realistic expectations, paper-traded by default.

What "good" looks like

At even (2.0) odds the break-even win rate is 50%. The model targets 54–58% on the bets it actually recommends — a thin but real edge that only pays off through volume and strict staking discipline, typically 2–8 value bets a day.

Risk-first by design

The system paper-trades by default — predictions and stakes are logged, not auto-wagered — so the strategy can be validated against live markets before any capital is committed.

No model guarantees profit; past performance does not predict future results. Built and operated as a personal quantitative-systems project, for jurisdictions where such activity is legal.

What's next

Where the edge gets sharper.

Closing-line value

Track each bet's price against the closing line — the strongest leading indicator of long-run edge, independent of short-term win/loss noise.

Bet earlier

Place into softer opening lines before the market sharpens, capturing more of the mispricing the model identifies.

Challenger-tour markets

Lower-tier tennis markets are thinner and less efficiently priced — fertile ground for a calibrated model with strong surface-aware features.

Live calibration monitoring

Surface model drift automatically from the MLflow history and trigger retraining when calibration slips.
